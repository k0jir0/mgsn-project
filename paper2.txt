A Trading Dashboard for a Small Crypto Token on the Internet Computer
======================================================================
Ryan V. — April 6, 2026


THE PROBLEM
-----------
When you own a small, illiquid cryptocurrency, there is almost no tooling
available to help you make good decisions. Large tokens like Bitcoin or
Ethereum have hundreds of charting platforms, price alerts, and analytical
services. Small tokens — especially those living on newer blockchains like
the Internet Computer Protocol (ICP) — have almost none. You are left
guessing: is now a good time to buy more? Am I overpaying? Would I make
more money just providing liquidity to the trading pool and collecting fees?

This project builds that missing tooling for MGSN, a small token on ICP,
and makes it available to anyone on the internet for free.


WHAT IS MGSN?
-------------
MGSN is a token that lives entirely on the Internet Computer, a blockchain
designed to run full software applications — not just financial transactions.
Its closest ecosystem companion is another token called BOB. The two trade
together on ICPSwap, which is the main decentralised exchange on ICP (think
of it like a vending machine for swapping one token for another, with no
company in the middle).

MGSN has a very small market cap and limited trading history. That makes it
both interesting and risky — small tokens can move dramatically in either
direction, and without good information, buying and selling becomes gambling.


WHAT THE PROJECT DOES
---------------------
The project is a two-page website deployed directly on the ICP blockchain.
The first page is a market dashboard tracking how MGSN and BOB have moved
over the past 22 months. The second page is the Strategy Engine: a system
that processes all available market data and tells you what to do with it.

The Strategy Engine answers three questions every time you open it:

  1. Should I buy, hold, or sell right now?
  2. If I am buying, how much should I put in?
  3. If I want to earn passive income from the trading pool, what should
     I expect to make?

It updates its answers automatically every 60 seconds using live price data
pulled directly from the ICPSwap exchange.


HOW THE BUY/SELL SIGNAL WORKS
-------------------------------
The engine does not use a single piece of information to make its
recommendation. It looks at six independent measures of the current market
and combines them into one score from 0 to 100.

Think of it like a doctor running six different tests before giving a
diagnosis. Any single test might be misleading; six tests pointing the same
way is much more reliable.

The six measures are:

  RSI — Is the token oversold or overbought?
  RSI (Relative Strength Index) tracks how strongly a price has moved
  recently. If a token has been falling for many months, its RSI drops
  toward zero — a sign it may be due to recover. If it has been rising
  strongly, the RSI climbs toward 100 — a sign it may be due to fall back.
  The engine watches RSI on BOB (the larger companion token) because BOB
  has more price history and the two tend to move together.

  SMA Crossover — Is momentum changing direction?
  Two moving averages are tracked: a fast one over 3 months and a slow one
  over 8 months. When the fast line crosses below the slow line, it usually
  means a downswing is underway — which is historically a good time to buy
  because prices are lower. When the fast line is well above the slow line,
  the market is already in a bull run and buying in is riskier.

  mNAV Ratio — Is MGSN cheap relative to its ecosystem?
  This compares the total value of all MGSN tokens in existence to the total
  value of all BOB tokens. If that ratio is far below its historical average,
  MGSN is cheap relative to the ecosystem it lives in — a value signal
  similar to buying a stock that is trading below its book value.

  ICP Cost Basis — How much ICP does one MGSN cost compared to history?
  Since most people buy MGSN using ICP, the engine tracks the MGSN/ICP
  exchange rate over time. If MGSN currently costs far fewer ICP than it
  has historically, it is on sale priced in the currency you would use to
  buy it.

  MACD — Is momentum accelerating or decelerating?
  The MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) tracks the gap between
  two moving averages. When this gap is negative but starting to close, it
  often means the market is bottoming out — one of the strongest early
  warning signs of a reversal. When the gap is wide and positive, the rally
  may already be mature.

  Bollinger Bands — How extreme is the current price versus recent history?
  Bollinger Bands draw a corridor around recent price activity. When the
  price touches the bottom of the corridor, it has moved unusually far in the
  bearish direction — statistically, it tends to bounce back. The engine
  measures exactly where the current price sits within this corridor.

Each of the six measures is converted into a score. They are blended with
different weights (RSI and mNAV carry more weight because they have been the
most reliable signals historically). The final composite score maps to a
clear action word:

  75–100 → STRONG BUY
   62–74 → BUY
   50–61 → ACCUMULATE
   38–49 → HOLD
    0–37 → REDUCE


HOW MUCH TO BUY: THE KELLY CRITERION
--------------------------------------
Even if the signal says "buy," you still need to decide how much to put in.
Investing too little misses the opportunity; investing too much risks wiping
out your capital if the signal is wrong.

The engine uses a formula called the Kelly Criterion to answer this question
mathematically. It looks at the 22-month history of BOB monthly returns —
specifically, how often months ended up (wins) versus down (losses), and by
how much on average. It then calculates the fraction of your available capital
that, if deployed each time the signal activates, would grow your money the
fastest in the long run.

Because this formula can occasionally suggest aggressive positions, the engine
applies "quarter-Kelly" — one quarter of the calculated value — as a safety
margin. The result is shown as a percentage, for example: "Put in 4.1% of
your liquid capital per entry."


THE BACKTEST: DOES THIS STRATEGY ACTUALLY WORK?
-------------------------------------------------
A backtest applies a strategy to historical data to see how it would have
performed. The engine runs a 22-month backtest comparing three approaches:

  Signal DCA — The strategy described above. Each month, you invest your
  usual budget, but the amount is multiplied up or down based on how strong
  the current signal is. During bear markets (when signals are strongest),
  you buy more. During bull markets, you buy less.

  Flat DCA — The same total budget, but split evenly every month regardless
  of market conditions.

  Lump Sum — All the money invested on day one.

Because MGSN does not have 22 months of stored price history yet, its price
is projected from the BOB series — if BOB was X% higher two years ago and
MGSN trades in correlation with BOB, MGSN was approximately X% higher too.
The resulting chart shows all three portfolio value lines over time alongside
the amount invested, revealing which strategy produced the best return on
the capital deployed.


PASSIVE INCOME: PROVIDING LIQUIDITY
-------------------------------------
Instead of simply buying and holding MGSN, you can deposit both MGSN and ICP
into the ICPSwap trading pool and earn a share of the 0.3% fee charged on
every trade that passes through. This is called liquidity provision (LP).

The engine estimates what this would earn you. You enter the total amount you
want to deposit and it shows:

  Conservative, base, and optimistic annual yield estimates based on
  expected trading volume in the MGSN/ICP pool.

  A table of impermanent loss — the hidden cost of LP that occurs when the
  price ratio between MGSN and ICP shifts. If MGSN triples against ICP while
  you are providing liquidity, you actually end up with fewer MGSN than if
  you had just held. The table quantifies this trade-off at five price scenarios.

  A compounding chart showing how fee income, if reinvested monthly back into
  the pool, grows over 12, 24, and 36 months.


THE ALERT BOARD AND ARBITRAGE DETECTOR
----------------------------------------
At the top of the strategy page, an alert board surfaces the two or three
most actionable insights at the current moment — in plain language. For
example: "BOB RSI at 28 — below 35, historically the optimal MGSN
accumulation zone." or "MACD histogram turning green — early reversal
signal on BOB."

Below the signals there is an arbitrage detector. It compares the current
ICPSwap live price of MGSN against the price that BOB's recent momentum
would imply MGSN should be worth right now. If the live price is more than
10% below the implied price, the market may be mispricing MGSN relative to
its ecosystem — a potential buying opportunity.


PORTFOLIO TRACKER
------------------
You can enter how many MGSN tokens you own and what you paid for them on
average. The engine then shows your unrealised profit or loss at the current
price, and calculates the value of your position if MGSN reaches each of
four milestone prices: $0.0001, $0.001, $0.01, and $0.10. These are simply
round numbers representing different stages of growth, not predictions.


WHERE IT LIVES
--------------
The entire website is deployed on the Internet Computer blockchain itself.
There is no hosting company, no server bill, and no single point of failure.
The code runs from a canister — a smart contract that serves web pages — and
is accessible at:

  Dashboard: https://yezrb-diaaa-aaaah-qugnq-cai.icp0.io/
  Strategy Engine: https://yezrb-diaaa-aaaah-qugnq-cai.icp0.io/strategy.html

The source code is public on GitHub at github.com/k0jir0/mgsn-project. The
strategy engine is a single JavaScript file about 1,400 lines long. It uses
no server-side code and requires no login. Trades are executed by the user
directly on ICPSwap using their own wallet.


WHAT THIS IS NOT
-----------------
This is a decision-support tool, not a guarantee of profit. The signals are
based on historical patterns in a 22-month dataset — a small sample — and
past performance does not predict future results. The Kelly Criterion gives a
mathematically principled position size, but the underlying market is
volatile and unpredictable. The best use of this engine is as one input
among many in a broader thinking process, not as an automated instruction
to follow blindly.
