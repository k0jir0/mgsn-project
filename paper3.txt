INTRODUCTION

A buyback program is one of the oldest and most reliable mechanisms for
increasing the value of an asset. Corporations have used stock buybacks for
decades. The principle is straightforward: fewer shares in circulation means
each remaining share represents a larger slice of the same underlying value.

The MGSN Buyback Program applies this same principle to a cryptocurrency token
on the Internet Computer blockchain. This paper explains the mechanics in full —
where the money comes from, how it is spent, what effect it has on the price,
and why it creates a self-reinforcing loop over time.

PART 1: WHERE THE MONEY COMES FROM

The buyback fund does not come from donations, inflation, or new token issuance.
It comes from trading fees.

MGSN trades on ICPSwap, a decentralized exchange (DEX) built on the Internet
Computer. Like all DEXes, ICPSwap operates through liquidity pools. A liquidity
pool is a smart contract that holds two tokens — in this case MGSN and ICP —
and allows anyone to swap between them at any time without a centralized order
book.

When a trader swaps ICP for MGSN, they pay a 0.3% fee on the transaction. That
fee does not go to a company. It goes directly to the liquidity providers (LPs)
— the people who deposited MGSN and ICP into the pool to make trading possible.

So the chain looks like this:

    Traders swap tokens
         ↓
    0.3% fee charged on every swap
         ↓
    Fee distributed proportionally to liquidity providers
         ↓
    50% of that fee income is pledged to the buyback fund

The buyback fund is therefore a direct function of trading volume. The more
MGSN is traded, the more fees accumulate, the more buying power flows into the
buyback program. It scales naturally with the activity of the market.

PART 2: WHAT HAPPENS WITH THAT MONEY

Once per month, the pledged fee income is collected and used to purchase MGSN
directly from the open market on ICPSwap.

This is a "market buy" — it executes at whatever the current market price is,
just like any other trader buying MGSN. The difference is that this buy is:

  1. Systematic — it happens on a fixed schedule regardless of price sentiment
  2. Transparent — every transaction ID is published in the public buyback log
  3. Self-funded — it does not dilute supply or rely on outside capital
  4. Permanent — the acquired MGSN is held or burned, never sold back

The phrase "market buy" is important. When a buy order enters the market, it
consumes the cheapest available MGSN from sellers. If the buyback spends $25,
it removes $25 worth of MGSN from the circulating supply. The sellers who
filled that order now hold USD (or ICP) instead of MGSN. The total MGSN
available for purchase on the open market is now smaller.

PART 3: WHY THIS INCREASES THE PRICE

Token price is determined by supply and demand. A buyback simultaneously
affects both sides of that equation.

ON THE SUPPLY SIDE:
Every buyback removes MGSN from circulating supply. MGSN has a fixed maximum
supply — no new tokens can ever be created. When tokens are acquired and held
(or burned), the total available on the market permanently shrinks. Supply
compression is the most direct form of price support because it changes the
fundamental scarcity of the asset. If demand stays constant and supply falls,
price must rise.

ON THE DEMAND SIDE:
Every monthly buyback is a guaranteed buy event. Markets are forward-looking.
Sophisticated buyers anticipate future demand and price it in early. When
participants know a systematic buyer will enter the market on a fixed schedule,
they are less likely to sell at a discount in the days leading up to it. This
creates a demand floor — a price level below which sellers are reluctant to go
because they know a buyer is coming.

ON MARKET PSYCHOLOGY:
Public buyback programs signal confidence. When the people closest to a project
(the liquidity providers and the team) are committing real money to buy the
token rather than sell it, it sends a credible signal to the market. This
reduces the "fear of holding" that suppresses price in small-cap tokens and
attracts longer-term holders who stabilize the price over time.

PART 4: THE COMPOUNDING LOOP

The most powerful feature of this design is that it compounds over time. Here
is why:

  Step 1: LPs add liquidity to the MGSN/ICP pool.
  Step 2: Larger pool → tighter price spread → more traders use the pool.
  Step 3: More trading → more fees collected.
  Step 4: More fees → larger monthly buyback.
  Step 5: Larger buyback → more supply removed → price rises.
  Step 6: Higher MGSN price → LP positions worth more → LPs earn more fees.
  Step 7: Return to Step 2, with a higher baseline.

Each cycle reinforces the next. This is not guaranteed — external market
conditions can interrupt any loop — but the structural incentive exists and
becomes stronger as the pool grows. Critically, the gains do not require new
participants at each stage. The same pool of liquidity, if trading volume
grows, generates larger and larger buybacks on its own.

PART 5: A WORKED NUMERICAL EXAMPLE

Let's use real numbers to make this concrete.

  Current pool TVL (total value locked):  $30,000
  Estimated monthly trading volume:       $15,000
  Pool fee rate:                           0.3%
  Monthly fee collected by pool:          $15,000 × 0.003 = $45

  Suppose you deposit $500 into the pool.
  Your share of the pool:                  $500 / $30,500 = 1.64%
  Your monthly fee income:                 $45 × 0.0164 = $0.74
  Your buyback pledge (50% of fees):       $0.37/month = $4.44/year

That sounds small — but the math scales linearly. A $50,000 deposit from a
single large LP would generate ~$74/month in fees and $37/month in buyback
pressure ($444/year). Across many LPs, those amounts aggregate into meaningful
and consistent market purchases.

In a scenario where monthly volume grows to $150,000 (10× growth — plausible
as MGSN gains visibility):

  Monthly fee collected:  $450
  Your share (at $500):   $0.74 × 10 = $7.40/month
  Buyback pledge:         $3.70/month = $44.40/year

The buyback contribution from the same $500 deposit grows tenfold purely from
increased trading activity, with no additional capital required from the LP.

PART 6: HOW IT DIFFERS FROM INFLATION-BASED TOKENOMICS

Many crypto projects attempt to increase token value through staking rewards or
yield farming, where new tokens are minted and distributed to holders. This
approach has a fundamental flaw: to pay rewards, the protocol must inflate
supply. Inflation is deflationary for price. If 10% of supply is minted each
year as rewards, the token must grow its market cap by 10% just to maintain the
same price. Most tokens fail this test, and the rewards are eroded by the very
inflation that funded them.

The MGSN buyback model works in the opposite direction:

  Inflation-based model:  mint tokens → distribute → supply grows → price diluted
  Buyback model:          collect fees → buy tokens → supply shrinks → price rises

No new MGSN is created. The buyback program is powered entirely by the economic
activity already happening around the token, redirected back into demand. This
makes it structurally sound and sustainable indefinitely, as long as trading
volume exists.

PART 7: TRANSPARENCY AND VERIFICATION

Every buyback executed under this program is recorded on the Internet Computer
blockchain. The transaction ID, date, USD amount spent, and MGSN acquired are
all published in the public buyback log on the MGSN Strategy Tracker website.

Anyone can verify:
  - The transaction exists on-chain using the ICP block explorer
  - The MGSN was purchased at the market price logged
  - The acquired MGSN is held in a public wallet address
  - No MGSN from the buyback pool has been sold back to the market

This transparency is not optional — it is the foundation of the program's
credibility. A buyback program that cannot be verified is merely a marketing
claim. The on-chain, timestamped, publicly auditable record transforms it into
a provable commitment.

SUMMARY

The MGSN Buyback Program increases token value through three simultaneous
mechanisms:

  1. SUPPLY REDUCTION — Purchased tokens are permanently removed from the
     market, compressing the float available to sellers.

  2. CONSISTENT DEMAND — Scheduled monthly market buys create a predictable
     demand floor, reducing the depth of price drawdowns.

  3. STRUCTURAL ALIGNMENT — LP incentives and holder incentives point in the
     same direction. LPs benefit from price appreciation, so their fee income
     funding buybacks benefits everyone in the ecosystem simultaneously.

The program does not require external capital, does not inflate supply, and
scales automatically with trading volume. Its effects are verifiable on-chain
and its logic is transparent. It is, in essence, the market's own activity
redirected to support itself.
